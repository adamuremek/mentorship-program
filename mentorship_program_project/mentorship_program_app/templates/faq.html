Mentorship Program FAQs

What is the Computer Science and Information Systems Mentorship System?

The mentorship system is an application that can be used to establish connections with SVSU CSIS students and professionals working with the Industrial Advisory Board in our area.

What is the Industrial Advisory Board?

The Industrial Advisory Board (IAB) is an organization that provides input and feedback on the academic programs offered by the SVSU CSIS department. Members of the IAB include Dow, Nexteer, Meta, Auto-Owners, Saginaw County, Dice Technoligies, Samsa and others.

What is the goal of the Mentorship System?

The mentorship system was created so that current SVSU students can talk with mentors who work in our field with the goal of helping students make informed decisions about their future career goals prior to graduation. Additionally, mentors will be there to support students through their undergraduate studies and difficulties that they may encounter.

What is a mentee?

A mentee is a current CSIS student enrolled in the mentorship system that can get a mentor to assist them with information about working experiences at local companies.

What is a mentor?

A mentor is an employee working for an IAB company that a mentee can talk to and gain skills, information, or experience from.

How do I get a mentee/mentor?

The system uses an algorithm based on shared interests and compatibility to pair mentors with mentees. The system will also allow mentors and mentees to search for mentorship opportunities based on company.

How do I contact my mentee/mentor?

Contact can be established through a listed email or other channels if preferred once the mentee/mentor relationship has begun.

Can I switch to a different mentor?

Yes, it could even be beneficial to work with multiple mentors to gain different experiences.
